AI Analysis

📊 This dashboard shows how your Meta (Facebook/Instagram) ads are performing. Each number tells a story about where your money is going and whether it's working. This guide explains every metric in plain language — no technical knowledge needed.

💰 SPEND & REACH
Amount Spent
How much money was used from your ad budget in the selected period. Higher doesn't always mean better — it matters how efficiently that money performed.
No benchmark — depends on your budget
Impressions
Total number of times your ads were shown on screen. If one person saw your ad 5 times, that counts as 5 impressions. This is your total visibility.
More impressions = more visibility. But watch frequency.
Reach
Number of unique people who saw your ad. Unlike impressions, seeing the same ad 5 times still counts as reach of 1. Shows how wide your audience spread is.
Higher reach with lower frequency is ideal.
Frequency
Average number of times each person saw your ad. Frequency of 3 means each person saw it ~3 times. Too high = ad fatigue — people start ignoring or hiding your ad.
Good: below 2.5 · Watch: 2.5–4 · Danger zone: above 4
👆 CLICKS & TRAFFIC
Link Clicks
Number of times people clicked the link in your ad to visit your website. This is the most important click metric — it shows real intent to visit your store.
Higher = better. Compare with LP Views to check site speed.
CTR — Click-Through Rate
Out of everyone who saw your ad, what % actually clicked it. Formula: Link Clicks ÷ Impressions × 100. Higher CTR = more compelling ad creative.
Good: above 1.5% · Average: 0.5–1.5% · Low: below 0.5%
CPC — Cost per Click
How much you paid on average for each link click. Lower is better. High CPC means your ad isn't engaging enough OR your audience is too competitive.
Good: below ₹15 · Average: ₹15–30 · High: above ₹30
CPM — Cost per 1,000 Impressions
How much it costs to show your ad 1,000 times. This reflects auction competition — if many advertisers target the same audience, CPM goes up. High CPM = expensive market.
Good: below ₹100 · Average: ₹100–200 · High: above ₹200
Landing Page Views (LP Views)
Number of times your website actually loaded after someone clicked. If Link Clicks = 100 but LP Views = 60, your site is slow — 40 people gave up waiting. Fix your site speed.
Should be close to Link Clicks. Drop-off above 20% needs attention.
🛒 FUNNEL — FROM INTEREST TO PURCHASE
Add to Cart
Number of people who added a product to their cart on your website after clicking the ad. Shows strong purchase intent — they liked what they saw.
Higher = better. Track ratio vs Purchases.
Checkouts Initiated
People who started the checkout process. They went beyond the cart to actually begin buying. Drop-off between ATC and Checkout usually means cart abandonment issues.
Should be 30–60% of Add to Carts.
Add Payment Info
People who entered their payment details. Very high intent — they were ready to pay. If this is high but Purchases are low, your payment process may have a bug or friction point.
Should be close to Checkouts Initiated.
Purchases
Final conversions — people who actually completed a purchase. This is the most important metric for an e-commerce brand. All other metrics support this one.
Higher = better. Directly tied to revenue.
Results
The primary goal of each campaign, defined by its objective. For a Sales campaign, Results = Purchases. For a Traffic campaign, Results = Link Clicks. It's the headline metric per campaign objective.
Depends on campaign objective.
Cost per Result / Cost per Purchase
How much you spent on average to get one purchase. If you spent ₹5,000 and got 10 purchases, Cost per Purchase = ₹500. Lower = more efficient ads.
Good: below ₹500 · Average: ₹500–1,500 · High: above ₹1,500
📊 CAMPAIGN OBJECTIVE
What does "Sales - Purchase" or "Sales - Add to Cart" mean?
This tells you what Meta is optimizing for — what action it's trying to get people to take.

Sales - Purchase: Meta shows your ad to people most likely to buy. Best for direct revenue.
Sales - Add to Cart: Meta shows your ad to people most likely to add to cart. Good for building pipeline but may not all convert to purchase.
Traffic: Meta shows your ad to people most likely to click and visit your site. Builds top-of-funnel.
Awareness: Max reach and visibility. People see your brand, not necessarily click.
📖 HOW TO USE THIS DASHBOARD
Daily routine (5 minutes)
1. Open the dashboard → set to Yesterday
2. Check Overview — look at CTR, CPC, Purchases, Cost/Purchase
3. Go to Benchmark tab — any red metrics need attention today
4. Go to Campaigns tab — which campaign is spending the most? Is it performing?
5. Click AI Recs → Generate Recommendations — get specific actions for today
6. Use Compare button to compare yesterday vs the day before
When to worry
🔴 CTR below 0.5% — Ad creative is not grabbing attention. Test new images/videos.
🔴 Frequency above 4 — Same people seeing your ad too many times. Expand audience or refresh creative.
🔴 LP Views much lower than Link Clicks — Website is too slow. People leaving before it loads.
🔴 High Add to Cart but low Purchases — Checkout has friction. Check price, shipping cost, payment options.
🔴 Cost per Purchase above ₹1,500 — Ads not efficient. Could be wrong audience, weak creative, or bad landing page.
